Name: Tuberculosis (TB)
Symptoms: Persistent cough, weight loss, night sweats, fever, chest pain.
Causes: Infection with Mycobacterium tuberculosis, airborne transmission.
Prevention: BCG vaccine, avoiding close contact with infected individuals, good ventilation.
Treatment: Antibiotics such as isoniazid and rifampicin taken over several months.
Diagnosis: Chest X-ray, sputum test, TB skin test.
Complications: Lung damage, spread to other organs, drug-resistant TB.
Contagious: Yes
Affected Population: People in crowded living conditions, immunocompromised individuals.
Type: Chronic
